Texas Nonprofits Partner to Bring Low-Income Homeowners Free Solar Energy

Austin Habitat for Humanity, The Texas Energy Poverty Research Institute and 1PlanetSol seek to bring free solar to Austin Habitat families.

Austin, TX – Austin Habitat for Humanity, The Texas Energy Poverty Research Institute (TEPRI), and 1PlanetSol announced their selection to participate in the U.S. Department of Energy SunShot Initiative’s Solar in Your Community Challenge, a $5 million prize competition that aims to expand solar electricity access to low- and moderate-income households.

“We are thrilled that our team was selected to join the challenge,” said Scott Nguyen, founder and Executive Director of 1PlanetSol. “We will install 20 free solar energy systems on low- and moderate-income households here in Austin, collectively saving these families $20,000 per year in electricity costs. In the process, we will demonstrate an innovative business model that can be easily scaled and replicated in communities nationwide.”

Over the next 18 months, the 1PlanetSol team will develop a new program that expands the benefit of solar to everyone by coupling solar to a local customer rewards program. Members of the program receive discounts on purchases at local Austin businesses while proceeds from membership fees fund the installation of solar on Austin Habitat for Humanity homes. The program will launch in early 2018 with an initial campaign to support 100 kilowatts of solar to benefit Austin Habitat families, and plans to include a study by TEPRI to capture the effects and implications of the program.

“The families we serve come out of situations where utility costs are a constant stressor,” said Phyllis Snodgrass, Chief Executive Officer of Austin Habitat for Humanity. “Being able to couple our affordable mortgage payments with sustainable and free solar energy will increase what our homeowners can spend on things such as education, healthcare and long-term financial stability.”

The 1PlanetSol team will join hundreds of other teams from around the country in their pursuit of solar projects and programs that expand solar access to low- and moderate-income households and non-profit organizations. All teams will compete for $1 million in final prizes, which will be awarded by judges based on each project or program’s innovation, impact and replicability.

Community members and organizations can join or help this new community-wide initiative by partnering and volunteering with 1PlanetSol. To find out ways to get involved, go to 1planetsol.org or email scott@1planetsol.org.
